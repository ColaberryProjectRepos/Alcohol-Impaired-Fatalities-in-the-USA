PROBLEM STATEMENT:


Alcohol physically changes the brain's chemistry and inhibits common sense, fear, and motor skills. If a driver's Blood Alcohol Concentration is above 0.10, they are seven times more likely than a sober driver to be involved in a fatal accident. In 2018, more than 27% of drivers with BAC levels of .08% or higher were between the ages of 21 to 25 and were involved in more fatal alcohol impaired driving crashes than any other age group. According to the National Highway Traffic Administration (NHTSA), Independence Day (July 4th) and Labor Day is the deadliest holidays, an average of 300 people died and 40% of those accidents occurred as the result of Alcohol impaired drivers.
